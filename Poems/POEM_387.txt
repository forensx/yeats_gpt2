Full of hope and dread Those two came carrying wine and meat and bread, And healed my wounds with unguents out of flowers, That feed white moths by some De Danaan shrine; Then in that hall, lit by the dim sea-shine, [204]We lay on skins of otters, and drank wine, Brewed by the sea-gods, from huge cups that lay Upon the lips of sea-gods in their day; And then on heaped-up skins of otters slept.
