We’ll play at chess Till the king come. It is but natural That she should fear him, for her house has been The hole of the badger and the den of the fox. If I were childish and had faith in omens I’d rather not have lit on that old chessboard At my homecoming. There’s a tale about it,— It has been lying there these many years,— Some wild old sorrowful tale. It is the board Where Lugaidh Redstripe and that wife of his Who had a seamew’s body half the year Played at the chess upon the night they died. I can remember now: a tale of treachery, A broken promise and a journey’s end. But it were best forgot.
