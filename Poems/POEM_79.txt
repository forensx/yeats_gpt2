The Maines’ children dropped their spades, and stood With quaking joints and terror-strucken faces, Till Maeve called out: ‘These are but common men. The Maines’ children have not dropped their spades, Because Earth, crazy for its broken power, Casts up a show and the winds answer it With holy shadows.’ Her high heart was glad, [49]And when the uproar ran along the grass She followed with light footfall in the midst, Till it died out where an old thorn tree stood.
